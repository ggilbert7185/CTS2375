Symptoms

Symptoms of COVID-19 range from mild symptoms to severe and life-threatening.
Please do not self-diagnose yourself!
If you have any of the symptoms listed on this page, please seek professional medical attention for an official diagnosis.
Due to the incubation period of the virus, symptoms may take 2-14 days to appear after exposure.

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath and/or difficulty breathing
  • Chills
  • Repeatedly shaking with chills
  • Muscle pain
  • Headache
  • Sore throat
  • New loss of taste and/or smell


If you have any of the following symptoms, please call 9-11 immediately! If you reside outside of the United States, please dial the number for your local emergency response service. These symptoms are considered emergency warning signs, and indicate that you need to seek medical attention immediately.

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion or inability to arouse
  • Bluish lips or face

*This list does not contain every single emergency warning sign. Please consult a medical professional for any symptoms that are severe or concerning.


Source: CDC - Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19): Symptoms of Coronavirus
